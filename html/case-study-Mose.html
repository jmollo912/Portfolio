mose.

Student Team Project

Year 2023
Duration 10 weeks

Overview

Mose is a platform designed to support caretakers in managing their responsibilities and finding community. The project focused on creating an empathetic, user-centered experience that addresses the unique challenges faced by those caring for loved ones.

Through user research and testing, I helped build a platform that provides both practical tools and emotional support for caretakers navigating their daily responsibilities.

My Roles

UX Designer: Building interface prototypes for Mose and defining core interaction mechanics to create a delightful, inclusive experience for families.
Industrial Designer: Developed and modeled Mose's physical product, aligning interaction goals with human ergonomics and branding.
Problem

Caretakers face numerous challenges managing their responsibilities, often feeling isolated and overwhelmed

Existing solutions were either too complex, lacked community support, or didn't address the emotional aspects of caregiving.

Key problems identified:

  • Lack of centralized tools for managing care schedules and medical information
  • Limited access to support communities and resources
  • Complex interfaces that were difficult to navigate during stressful situations
  • Insufficient emotional support and connection with other caretakers
  • Poor mobile experience for on-the-go care management
Insights

Understanding daily challenges, emotional needs, and market gaps through research

I conducted in-depth interviews with caretakers from diverse backgrounds and analyzed existing care management platforms to identify opportunities.

User Research

I conducted in-depth interviews with caretakers from diverse backgrounds to understand their daily challenges, emotional needs, and goals. These conversations revealed that caretakers needed simple, accessible tools and strong community connections to feel supported.

Competitive Analysis

I analyzed existing care management platforms and support communities to identify gaps and opportunities. This research helped us understand what was missing in the market and how we could create a more comprehensive solution.

User Testing Findings

Throughout the design process, I conducted multiple rounds of user testing with caretakers to validate design decisions. Testing showed that simplified interfaces, clear information hierarchy, and easy access to community features significantly improved user satisfaction and engagement. Users appreciated the empathetic approach and found the platform genuinely supportive.

Solution

A supportive platform that simplifies care management and connects caretakers

The dashboard provides a clear overview of responsibilities and schedules, while community features connect caretakers with others who understand their experiences.

Care Management Dashboard

The dashboard provides caretakers with a clear overview of their responsibilities, schedules, and important information. Key features are easily accessible, and the interface is designed to be calming and supportive rather than overwhelming.

Community Support

Recognizing the importance of emotional support, I designed community features that connect caretakers with others who understand their experiences. These features include discussion forums, support groups, and resource sharing.

Key Features

The platform includes streamlined care management tools, community connections, resource libraries, and accessible design that works for users of all technical abilities. The empathetic design approach ensures that caretakers feel supported and understood throughout their journey.
